Imoni(芋煮) - Yamagata version -

Do you want to know the best hot pot dish in Japan? Today, I will tell you about the special one which is one of the traditional & popular hot pots in Tohoku region (= northeast part of Japan)! It is called "Imoni (simmered taro)", which is a soysauce based soup dish with sliced beef and some veggies. We often have the imoni party every fall in the park or riverside. Reference recipe website: kurashiru(クラシル)

By: Megumi Akama

Recipe Quick Facts

Active: 30 mins

Total: 35 mins

Servings: 2

Ingredients

Directions

Step 1

Cut off the top and bottom of the taro, peel and cut into bite-sized pieces.

Step 2

Tear konjac into bite-sized pieces with a spoon, and slice green onions into 1 cm pieces.

Step 3

Pour water, #1 and half of the seasonings(sugar, soysause, sake) into a pot and heat over medium heat.

Step 4

When taro is well cooked, add #2 and beef slices. When the beef slices are cooked, add the remaining seasonings and simmer over medium heat for 5 minutes, then remove from heat.

[Optional] If the soup left enough....

Add Udon noodles and curry powder! You can enjoy "curry udon" as well.
